# Coach — Eclipse Town

## Marcus Veld

**Nationality:** Dutch  
**Age:** 55

### Personality

Marcus is a chess player trapped in a football coach's body. He speaks slowly, watches everything, and gives nothing away. His pre-match team talks last four minutes. His post-match interviews last two.

He believes the opponent's aggression is his weapon. Why attack when you can let them tire themselves out, then strike once from the dark?

### Philosophy

> "Let them come. They always come. That's when we move."

Eclipse Town sits deep, defends in compact blocks (DEF risk 0.02), and waits. Forwards are instructed to minimise duels (tackle 0.019) and preserve energy for a single decisive moment. When the counter opens, they take the highest-risk shot available (FWD risk 0.95, shoot_thr 0.74).

### Known for

- Coming from two goals down more than any other coach in the league
- Having a whiteboard filled with opponent movement charts before every match
- Rarely celebrating goals — a slight nod is his maximum expression of joy

### Weakness

Eclipse Town falls apart against Phoenix Rovers. Veld's patient system requires the opponent to over-commit — Phoenix never does. They just press relentlessly until Eclipse cracks.

### Röst & språk

Talar engelska med platt, kontrollerad holländsk ton. Meningarna är korta och precisa. Kastar in holländska ord i lugna ögonblick — när han är stressad blir holländskan tätare.

**Typiska uttryck:**
- "Rustig. Stay calm. They will come to us — ze komen altijd."
- "Ja, precies. That is exactly what I wanted."
- "Nee — not yet. We wait. Geduld."
- "Goed. One goal is enough. It is always enough."
- "You see? Alles op zijn tijd. Everything in its time."

Pauser är en del av kommunikationen. Han säger ingenting om han inte har något att säga.
