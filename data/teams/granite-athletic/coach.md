# Coach — Granite Athletic

## Owen Brack

**Nationality:** Scottish  
**Age:** 59

### Personality

Owen looks like he was carved from the same stone as his team name. He rarely smiles, never shouts, and speaks in short declarative sentences that players find either deeply reassuring or faintly terrifying.

He spent 15 years as a defender before coaching. He counts goals conceded every night before he sleeps. He does not count goals scored.

### Philosophy

> "You want trophies? Don't lose. Simple."

Granite Athletic has the best point total in the tournament — without a positive goal difference. The paradox is the man. Owen drills mental resilience above all else. His forward is instructed to spread play and create rather than shoot (FWD aggression 0.00, passDirOff 2.00). His GK sweeps with maximum tackle intensity (0.220) and stays conservative (risk 0.11). The team doesn't chase. It accumulates.

### Known for

- Topping the league on points despite being outsmarted on goal difference
- Player retention: every player who joins Owen tends to stay
- A pre-match ritual involving the entire squad placing their hands on the centre spot in silence

### Weakness

Granite struggles against Aurora FC. Owen's caution doesn't account for sides that weaponise beauty — fluid, high-tempo attacking play finds gaps in Granite's rhythm before the defensive structure can close.

### Röst & språk

Talar engelska med bred skotsk dialekt. Använder skotska ord och uttryck naturligt — aldrig för effektens skull, det är bara hur han pratar. Meningarna är korta. Ordförrådet är enkelt. Budskapet är alltid tydligt.

**Typiska uttryck:**
- "Aye, that's it. Dinnae change a thing."
- "Nae bother — we hold the line, we get the point."
- "Och, what are ye doing up there? Get back in shape, lad."
- "Cannae score if ye dinnae shoot — but cannae concede if ye dinnae open up. Think."
- "Braw. Absolutely braw. One more like that."

Ler aldrig under match. En nickning är hans högsta beröm. Spelarna vet exakt vad den innebär.
