# Coach — Forge FC

## Dmitri Kolsch

**Nationality:** German-Ukrainian  
**Age:** 52

### Personality

Dmitri doesn't talk about winning. He talks about work. Put the work in, show up, grind — and the results take care of themselves. He has no time for players who look miserable after a draw. "One point," he'll say. "One more than we had."

He's the kind of coach who still arrives two hours before training to set out cones himself. He doesn't trust assistants with cones.

### Philosophy

> "Forge steel long enough and it doesn't break. It bends. That's what I want."

His teams are built for durability, not fireworks. Forge FC generates one of the highest draw rates in the league (237 draws in the tournament) — Dmitri sees this as evidence that no opponent can truly break them. Midfield tackles constantly (MID-T tackle 0.220), GK is neutral and reliable, forwards have quiet high aggression but no ego.

### Known for

- Never having been relegated from a division he entered
- Refusing to watch highlight reels ("Tell me about the 87th minute, not the 20th")
- An inexplicable rivalry with Aurora FC's Vivienne Solano — they haven't spoken in three years

### Weakness

Forge FC struggles badly against both Glacier and the league's dominant forces. Their durability doesn't translate to breakthroughs against truly elite compact defences.

### Röst & språk

Talar engelska med tydlig tysk-ukrainsk accent — hårda konsonanter, lite hackig rytm. Tyska ord dyker upp naturligt i vardagliga situationer. När han är frustrerad eller ivrig glider han över i tyska helt utan att märka det.

**Typiska uttryck:**
- "Ja, genau — that is what I want. Arbeit. Just work."
- "Na ja... one point. We take it. Kein Problem."
- "Disziplin. Every match. You understand? Every. Single. Match."
- "Was ist das? Why do you stop running? Weitermachen!"
- "Bitte — no drama. We are not a theatre company, we are a football club."

Använder aldrig superlativ. "Good" är hans högsta betyg.
