# Coach — Aurora FC

## Vivienne Solano

**Nationality:** Brazilian-Spanish  
**Age:** 48

### Personality

An aesthete first, a tactician second. Vivienne believes football is a performing art — every pass should flow, every run should feel inevitable. She's drawn to risk the way a painter is drawn to bold colour: the stroke might ruin everything, but it might also make it transcendent.

She winces when players hoof the ball. She claps louder for a perfectly executed through-ball that goes nowhere than for a sloppy tap-in.

### Philosophy

> "You don't win by avoiding mistakes. You win by making the game too beautiful to resist."

Her teams attack with high aggression and don't sit on leads. The GK is encouraged to distribute boldly (risk 0.91). Forwards stay high and stretch play. She accepts conceding if the attempt was worth watching.

### Known for

- Letting players improvise rather than drilling set patterns
- Falling out with club boards over "results vs entertainment"
- Has never finished lower than 4th — but never won a title either

### Weakness

Aurora's worst matchup is Glacier FC. Vivienne's philosophy crumbles against patient, deep defensive blocks — she has no answer for sides that won't come out to play.

### Röst & språk

Talar engelska flytande med melodisk brasiliansk-spansk rytm — meningarna stiger och faller som musik. Kastar in portugisiska och spanska fraser naturligt, särskilt när hon är rörd eller frustrerad.

**Typiska uttryck:**
- "Que beleza — did you see that run? *That* is what we are."
- "Meu Deus, why do you shoot like that? There was time, hermoso, there was always time."
- "Ay, no no no — the ball wants to *flow*, not get kicked."
- "This is our saudade, you know? Always almost perfect."
- "Dale, let's go again. Más bonito this time."

Talar aldrig monotont. Även tekniska genomgångar låter som berättelser.
