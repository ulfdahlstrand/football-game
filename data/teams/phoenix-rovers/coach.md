# Coach — Phoenix Rovers

## Rafael Toro

**Nationality:** Argentine  
**Age:** 46

### Personality

Rafael does not have an indoor voice. His touchline presence — arms wide, voice cutting across noise, expression somewhere between fury and rapture — is as much a part of Phoenix Rovers as anything on the pitch.

He believes intensity is a skill you develop through repetition. His training sessions run long. His players are among the fittest in the league. Some say he's relentless. He considers that a compliment.

### Philosophy

> "Don't save energy. Energy creates energy."

Phoenix Rovers attack with every line. Defenders press high (DEF aggression 1.89, tackle 0.219), the GK reads aggressively (aggr 1.50), and the team generates very few draws (only 95) because they never settle. The MID plays forward at maximum passDirOff (1.99). They outrun their opponents in the final 20 minutes.

### Known for

- The highest come-from-behind win rate in the league
- Post-match talks where he replays every decisive moment in real time, on his feet
- Beating Mirage SC — Rafael's intensity disrupts Yasmin Fadel's choreography completely

### Weakness

Glacier FC. Sigrid Holt's deep defensive patience absorbs Phoenix's aggression without breaking. The more Rafael's side press, the more compact and comfortable Glacier become. It is the one scenario where intensity becomes a liability.

### Röst & språk

Talar engelska med argentinsk spanska i blodet — snabbt, musikalt, med hårda *r* och mjuka *ll*. Spanska ord och fraser briserar fram när han är upprymd eller frustrerad, ibland mitt i en engelsk mening. Kallar spelarna *hermano* eller *che* som ett reflexmässigt tilltalsord.

**Typiska uttryck:**
- "Dale, dale — go! Keep going, hermano, don't stop!"
- "Che, what was that? You had the space — *tenías todo*! Everything!"
- "Vamos, vamos — we don't stop in the 70th minute, we start there."
- "Claro que sí — of course it hurts. That is how you know you are alive."
- "Boludo, I love you — but run faster. *Más rápido*, por favor."

Tystas aldrig ner av ett bakslag. Varje förlust är bara bränsle till nästa match.
